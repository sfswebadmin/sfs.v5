Happily, the last few decades have done much for queer acceptance in parent-child relationships – it’s quietly vital to the mother-son bond in the Irish comedy Four Mothers ... Irish director Darren Thornton’s delightful film is a reworking of the 2008 Italian charmer ‘Mid-August Lunch’, and is in many respects an improvement …

Four Mothers is droll and gentle, but in its own way markedly progressive: films about queer adults still negotiating their identities with their elderly parents are unusual, with or without this one’s delicacy and wit.

<div class='review__author review__author--review1'>Guy Lodge, The Observer</div>

This enjoyable Irish comedy drama follows Edward (James McArdle), an up-and-coming gay novelist and a full-time carer for his 80-something mother (Fionnula Flanagan), who communicates via an iPad after a stroke …

… this briskly paced film from writer/director Darren Thornton treats its elderly characters with compassion and doesn't ignore the contradictory feelings one might experience in caring for an ageing parent. Blending humour and insight, Four Mothers features a fine ensemble that expertly brings out its characters' different personalities, beliefs and life experiences.

<div class='review__author'>Tom Dawson, Radio Times</div>

### Film Facts

- Written by brothers Darren and Colin Thornton, this is a touching story inspired by real life.
- Four Mothers had 7 nominations for the Irish Film & Television Awards
- Fionnula Flanagan was also in the film ‘Four Brothers’ in 2005
