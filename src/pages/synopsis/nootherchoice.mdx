“There is something so rewarding about going to a movie and giving yourself over to a master like Park Chan-wook, someone whom you trust through all the twists and turns of a film as tonally complex as “No Other Choice.”

It’s so easy to see all of the places where this unique gem could have gone wrong, and so satisfying to see it only make good choices from beginning to end.”

<div class='review__author review__author--review1'>Roger Ebert.com</div>

“Park Chan-wook’s new film brings his usual effortlessly fluent, steely confidence and a type of storytelling momentum that can accommodate all kinds of digressions, set-pieces and the occasional trance-like submission to mysterious visions. There are sub-plots of family disarray, which loom out of the screen quite as importantly as the bizarre homicide campaign.

And throughout it all, a deadpan note of knockabout black comedy is never entirely absent. What on earth does it all mean?”

<div class='review__author'>Peter Bradshaw, Guardian</div>

### Film Facts

- Based on The Ax, a USA novela, but now set in Korea
- World premiere at the 82<sup>nd</sup> Venice International Film Festival
- Multiple award nominations at the 83<sup>rd</sup> Golden Globe Awards
