“From Hilde, With Love doesn't rely on grand spectacle or heavy-handed drama to make its point. Instead, it quietly immerses us in the final months of
resistance fighter Hilde Coppi's life, finding strength in understatement and human detail.

Set against the backdrop of Nazi-era Berlin, the film explores themes of defiance, love, and sacrifice through a restrained yet emotionally resonant lens.

Fries gives a wonderfully nuanced performance, her expressive eyes working overtime. She expertly uncovers the strength and resilience behind Hilde’s shyness, sliding from pinched awkwardness with the resistance group to fierce love for her fragile baby son Hans Jr, born after a tense, life-threatening labour in prison.

As her baby’s hand pats her face, you can see Hilde’s principled resistance to her captors warring with her longing to stay with him at any price.

<div class='review__author'>Mia Pflüger, Picturehouse</div>

### Film Facts

- The term Red Orchestra was coined by the Gestapo, who referred to the German resistance radio operators as "pianists" and their supervisors as "conductors."
- This disparate network consisted of over 150 Berlin Nazi opponents from various backgrounds, including artists, scientists, workers, and students, who were united by their rejection of National Socialism.
- The couple’s son, Hans Coppi Jr., was raised by his paternal grandparents and became a historian dedicated to preserving the memory of German resistance to the Nazi regime.
